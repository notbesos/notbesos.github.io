series

arithmetic

An arithmetic series is just an initial number with a common difference which is added for every next term.

initial term: common difference:

Terms:

A general formula for the nth term of an arithmetic series is:

t(n) = n1 + (n - 1)d

Where n1 is the initial term and d is the common difference.

example problems

Problem 1: An arithmetic series has 10 terms. The fourth term is 24 and the tenth term is 96. What is the series?

We know that the 4th term is 24 so,

24 = n1 + (4 - 1)d
24 = n1 + 3d

We also know that the 10th term is 96 so,

96 = n1 + (10 - 1)d
96 = n1 + 9d

We can then solve this as a system of equations.

- 96 = n1 + 9d 24 = n1 + 3d

72 = 6d

d = 12

With the common difference we can now solve for n1.

24 = n1 + 3(12)
24 = n1 + 36
n1 = -12

Therefore the series is,

t(n) = 12(n - 1) - 12

Problem 2: Peter likes to collect rocks and he collects 7 rocks every day. If he has 102 rocks on week 2, how many rocks did he have on day 3?

The common difference is given, 7 rocks per day, and we also know how many rocks he has on day 14, 102.

102 = n1 + (14 - 1)7
102 = n1 + (13)7
102 = n1 + 91
n1 = 11

So the formula for this series is,

t(n) = 11 + (n - 1)7

Finally, we solve for the formula where n is 3.

t(3) = 11 + (3 - 1)7
t(3) = 11 + (2)7
t(3) = 11 + 14
t(3) = 25

The solution is 25 rocks.

geometric

A geometric series is like an arithmetic series but instead of adding by a common difference, we multiply by a common ratio.

initial term: common ratio:

Terms:

A general formula for a geometric series is

t(n) = n1 × rn - 1

Where n1 is the initial term and r is the common ratio.

example problems

Problem 3: A geometric series has 13 terms. The second term is 6 and the last term is 12288. What is the series?

We know that the 2nd term is 6.

6 = n1 × r2 - 1
6 = n1 × r1

We also know that the 13th term is 12288.

12288 = n1 × r13 - 1
12288 = n1 × r12

We can reorganize the equations in order to solve a system of equations.

12288 = n1 × r12
n1 = 12288 ÷ r12
6 × r12 = 12288 × r1
6 × r11 = 12288
r11 = 2048
r = 2

With the common ratio, r we can find the initial term.

6 = n1 × 21
6 = n1 × 2
n1 = 3

So the series is:

t(n) = 3 × 2n - 1

Problem 4: Hoover likes to build model planes. Every week Hoover builds three times the amount of planes the previous week
If Hoover builds a single plane on week one, by week 6, how many planes has he built?

We are given the common ratio, 3 and the initial term, 1. We can construct the formula for the series.

t(n) = 1 × 3n - 1

Then we can replace n with 6, the number of weeks, and find out the amount of planes.

t(3) = 1 × 36 - 1
t(3) = 1 × 35
t(3) = 1 × 243
t(3) = 243

Hoover has built 243 planes by week 6.

pascal's triangle

construction of the triangle

To construct Pascal's triangle you start with the number 1 on the first row.

To make each additional row, you add the two numbers to the left and right on the previous row. In case the number does not exist, it should be treated as a zero.

using the triangle to expand (a + b)n

Expanding out (a + b)n by hand gets very difficult very quickly, however Pascal's triangle can do most of the work for us.

Each row of the triangle gives us the factors of the terms of the binomial expansion.
